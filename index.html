Executive Summary

This executive summary presents the key findings from three comprehensive research studies conducted in 2024-2025 to assess Abu Dhabi's domestic brand health. These insights form the foundation for the strategic recommendations in the full report.

Research Sources

  • UAE Brand Tracker (Q4'24) - Quarterly survey of 800 UAE residents outside Abu Dhabi
  • Destination Visitor Survey (DVS) 2024 - Face-to-face interviews with 2,500 domestic visitors
  • Nightlife Survey 2024 - Online survey of 1,500 UAE residents focused on nightlife preferences

Brand Health Metrics

Record-High Performance

Abu Dhabi is experiencing its highest brand health metrics on record, with significant improvements over the past year:

  • Total ad awareness: 73% (+8% YoY)
  • Consideration to visit: 75% (+7% YoY)
  • Intent to visit: 57% (+4% YoY)
  • Past 12-month visitation: 58% (+3% YoY)

The emirate has shown the most consistent growth pattern among all emirates, with visitation increasing from 32% in Q3'22 to 58% in Q4'24.

Marketing Funnel Effectiveness

Abu Dhabi shows strong performance throughout the marketing funnel:

  • Awareness: 73% of UAE residents are aware of Abu Dhabi advertising
  • Consideration: 75% would consider visiting Abu Dhabi in the next 6 months
  • Intent: 57% plan to visit Abu Dhabi in the next 3 months
  • Action: 58% have actually visited Abu Dhabi in the past 12 months

The high conversion rate from consideration to action (77% - calculated from 58% visitation vs 75% consideration) indicates that Abu Dhabi's marketing is particularly effective at converting interest into actual visits, outperforming other emirates in this regard.

Brand Perception Strengths

Abu Dhabi's strongest brand associations:

  • A place to reconnect with oneself: 72%
  • Hosting exciting international sports events: 71%
  • Beautiful beaches: 70%
  • Interesting museums: 69%
Brand Perception Challenges

Areas where Abu Dhabi has weaker associations:

  • Good weather year-round: 37%
  • Inspiring experiences: 42%
  • Easy to access: 43%
  • Accommodation options for every budget: 43%

Visitor Insights

Short Stay Duration

The average length of stay for domestic visitors is just 1.2 days, with 67% being same-day visitors (up from 50% in 2021). This represents a significant opportunity to convert these visitors into overnight stays.

Visitor Demographics
  • 57% of visitors are between 18-39 years of age
  • 62% are male
  • 96% come from either Dubai (70%) or Sharjah (26%)
  • Business visitors account for 35% of all domestic visitors, with higher proportions among same-day visitors (53%)
Information Sources

Primary information sources for visitors:

  • Social media: Instagram (61%), Facebook (60%), TikTok (44%)
  • Online search engines: 59%
  • Personal recommendations: 55%

57% of domestic visitors don't typically make any bookings for their trips, highlighting the spontaneous nature of domestic travel.

Declining Revisit Intent

While 82% express a strong desire to revisit, this has been declining over the past three years (from 92% in 2021), driven by decreases among business and VFR (Visiting Friends and Relatives) visitors.

Nightlife Insights

Nightlife "Leakage"

55% of Abu Dhabi residents visit Dubai for nightlife activities, spending significantly more per person (AED 591 vs. AED 436) and often staying overnight (66%). This represents a substantial opportunity to enhance Abu Dhabi's nightlife offerings.

Nightlife Demographics

Nightlife is particularly important to:

  • Emiratis (78% importance) with 75% "leakage" to Dubai
  • Westerners (77% importance) with 92% "leakage" to Dubai
  • The 18-35 age group (62-63% "leakage" rates)
Nightlife Motivators

Factors that would motivate more nightlife participation in Abu Dhabi:

  • Wider variety of venues: 48%
  • More vibrant atmosphere: 46%
  • More affordable venues: 42%
  • Unique experiences: 35%
  • Better quality entertainment: 31%
Campaign Effectiveness

The Q4 2024 campaigns have proven highly effective:

  • Global Brand Ambassador (Chris Hemsworth): 49% recognition rate with 65% correct brand association and exceptional 91% appeal rating
  • Cultural Heritage Campaign: 31% definite recognition with 74% correct brand association and strong 88% appeal rating

Both campaigns significantly outperform tourism industry benchmarks (typically 30-35% recognition).

Seasonal Patterns

Q2 consistently shows the strongest growth for Abu Dhabi awareness and visitation, suggesting seasonal factors influence campaign effectiveness. The most significant jumps in awareness occurred between Q1'23 and Q2'23 (from 27% to 39% spontaneous awareness).
